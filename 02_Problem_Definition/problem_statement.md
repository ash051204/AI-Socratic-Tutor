## Problem Statement

Despite significant advancements in automatic question generation and the growing use of large language models in education, existing systems still face major limitations when applied to real learning scenarios.

Most question generation systems are designed to produce questions from text, but they operate in isolation without engaging in meaningful interaction with students. These systems do not consider student responses, provide follow-up questions, or guide learners step-by-step toward deeper understanding.

On the other hand, conversational AI systems improve interaction but often lack structured questioning strategies and do not follow a pedagogical framework. They tend to provide direct answers instead of encouraging students to think and reason.

Additionally, adaptive learning — the ability to adjust question difficulty based on student performance — is either missing or implemented in a limited and static way. Existing systems do not dynamically adapt in real time based on how well a student is understanding the concept.

As a result, there is currently no integrated system that combines:
- Structured Socratic questioning
- Adaptive learning based on Bloom's Taxonomy
- Conversational AI interaction

Therefore, the problem addressed in this project is:

How can we design an AI-based teaching assistant that engages students in a structured Socratic dialogue, adapts to their level of understanding in real time, and promotes deep conceptual learning instead of passive knowledge consumption?
